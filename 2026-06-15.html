Basic Immunology 2 papers

Enteropathy in STAT3 GOF syndrome: Insights into the role of the JAK-STAT pathway

Caranfil, Crepaldi, Marzollo, … Zingone et al. · Autoimmunity Reviews, 19 May 2026 · review

Key findings

  • JAK-STAT3 transduces signals from many pro-inflammatory cytokines; uncontrolled activation fuels inflammatory and autoimmune disease, lymphoma and solid tumors.
  • STAT3 gain-of-function mutations cause a rare syndrome of villous-atrophy enteropathy plus lymphoproliferation, autoimmune cytopenias and growth delay.
  • Expands the spectrum of non-celiac (seronegative) atrophic enteropathies driven by JAK-STAT alterations.
  • An emblematic case shows anti-JAK therapy is effective — positioning JAK-STAT3 inhibition as a promising targeted option.

A longitudinal atlas of human psoriatic skin reveals the mechanisms of anti-IL-23 therapy in disrupting the type 17 inflammatory circuit

Jiang, Blauvelt, Shi, … Gudjonsson et al. · Immunity, 28 May 2026

Key findings

  • scRNA-seq and spatial transcriptomics on matched pre-/post-treatment psoriatic skin, followed 36 weeks after stopping IL-23 blockade.
  • IL-23 blockade durably reduces IL-17–producing CD8 tissue-resident memory T cells via a c-MAF–IL-7 axis and suppresses IL-17 T-cell infiltration.
  • It reduces APOE⁺/IL34⁺ fibroblasts; lowering fibroblast-derived IL-34 cuts monocyte→dendritic-cell differentiation and pro-inflammatory DCs.
  • Reduced vascular CCL19/CCL21 attenuates infiltration — stable remodeling that breaks the inflammatory loop and explains lasting efficacy.

Basic Immuno-metabolism 1 paper

Secondary bile acids as immune and metabolic mediators

Fiorucci, Biagioli, Marchianò, Zampella, Distrutti · Trends in Immunology, 3 Mar 2026 · review

Key findings

  • Bile acids are cholesterol-derived ligands made by combined human and bacterial genes, acting on many receptors and transcription factors body-wide.
  • The liver makes only two primary bile acids; the gut microbiota generates hundreds of secondary and microbially conjugated species (DCA, LCA, isoallo-LCA).
  • Secondary bile acids regulate systemic immune function, promote thyroid-hormone (T4→T3) conversion, and set energy expenditure in muscle and adipose.
  • They mediate the beneficial effects of calorie restriction on healthspan/longevity, signaling via FXR and GPBAR1/TGR5 — with roles in immune disorders.

HIV 1 paper

TGF-β mediates epigenetic control of innate antiviral responses and SIV reservoir size

Ghneim, Ten-Caten, Santana, … Sharma, Paiardini, Soudeyns, Pereira Ribeiro, Sekaly et al. · Nature Immunology, 20 Mar 2026

Key findings

  • In SIV-infected macaques given anti-IL-10 + anti-PD-1, epigenetic programming of myeloid and CD4 T cells drives decay of cell-associated viral DNA after treatment interruption.
  • TGF-β/SMAD signaling suppresses innate antiviral activity through HDACs, reducing chromatin accessibility of IRFs and STATs.
  • Adding HDAC inhibitors in vitro restores antiviral responses despite TGF-β; IL-6 amplifies the network through IRF9.
  • An overlapping cascade operates in HIV elite controllers — nominating epigenetic levers for cure strategies.

Aging 1 paper

The placental metabolic clock

Yoshioka & Imai · Science, 11 Jun 2026 · perspective

Key findings

  • Commentary on evidence that NAD⁺ depletion in the placenta triggers a metabolic "countdown" to birth in mice.
  • Links a declining placental NAD⁺/metabolic state to the timing of parturition.
  • Frames a placental "metabolic clock," connecting NAD biology (a geroscience theme) to the control of birth timing.

Innovations in Bioinformatics 1 paper

CytoSignal detects locations and dynamics of ligand-receptor signaling at cellular resolution from spatial transcriptomic data

Liu, Manabe, Qian, … Ono, Welch et al. · Nature Genetics, 10 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • CytoSignal infers the locations and dynamics of ligand–receptor signaling at cellular resolution from spatial transcriptomic data.
  • Spatially resolved signaling scores reveal gradients, distinguish contact-dependent from diffusible interactions, and surface signaling-associated genes across multi-sample data.
  • It predicts the temporal dynamics of an interaction at each spatial location.
  • Validated in situ by proximity ligation assay, localizing LR interactions more accurately than previous approaches.
