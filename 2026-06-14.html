Basic Immunology 7 papers

Atlas of human gut-associated lymphoid tissue reveals immunomodulatory interactions of B cells

Pitcher, Sun, Dionisi, … Spencer et al. · Science Immunology, 5 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • Integrated multiomics builds a spatially resolved atlas of human gut-associated lymphoid tissue (GALT) and its B-cell subsets.
  • Double-negative-2 (DN2) B cells show broad regulatory features with potential to maintain homeostasis in microbe-rich mucosa.
  • In ulcerative colitis, GALT B-cell composition and spatial distribution are distorted with altered immunomodulatory potential.

Human vaccine responses regulated by parallel cytokine pathways

Chen, Guo, Heath, … Davis et al. · Nature Immunology, 12 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • Across four influenza-vaccine cohorts over five seasons (n=581), baseline serum IL-18 and IFN-β correlated with day-28 antibody responses.
  • In human tonsil/spleen organoids, type I IFNs, IL-21 and IL-12 (not IL-18 or IFNγ) enhanced antibody production; IL-12 induced IL-21 in humans, unlike mice.
  • mRNA-LNP delivery of IL-21 or IFN-β promoted long-lived plasma cells — a human-centric platform for adjuvant discovery.

A longitudinal atlas of human psoriatic skin reveals the mechanisms of anti-IL-23 therapy in disrupting the type 17 inflammatory circuit

Jiang, Blauvelt, Shi, … Gudjonsson et al. · Immunity, 28 May 2026

Key findings

  • scRNA-seq and spatial transcriptomics on matched pre/post psoriatic skin, tracked 36 weeks after IL-23 blockade was stopped.
  • IL-23 blockade durably reduces IL-17⁺ CD8 tissue-resident memory T cells via a c-MAF–IL-7 axis and lowers APOE⁺/IL34⁺ fibroblasts, cutting monocyte-to-DC differentiation.
  • Reduced vascular CCL19/CCL21 attenuates immune-cell infiltration — mapping how IL-23 inhibition breaks the type-17 loop for lasting remission.

A cell-intrinsic glucocorticoid biosynthesis and sensing circuit maintains a homeostatic Th17 cell state

Yang, Huang, Cheng, … Anderson et al. · Immunity, 12 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • Homeostatic vs pathogenic Th17 cells are distinguished by differential glucocorticoid (GC) production downstream of the steroidogenic enzyme CYP11A1.
  • TCR signaling opens the Cyp11a1 locus; TGF-β1 and IL-6 maintain it, and Th17-derived GC signals through a highly expressed GR in a self-reinforcing loop.
  • Multi-omics defines the gene network, and TGF-β1 can restore GC sensing — relevant to treating Th17-mediated autoimmunity.

Interleukin 23 promotes a pro-inflammatory Th17 cell state by stabilizing RORγt and suppressing glucocorticoid receptor activity

Yang, Huang, Yang, … Kuchroo et al. · Immunity, 12 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • Phosphoproteomic + transcriptomic profiling downstream of IL-23R (vs the subunit-sharing IL-12R) reveals two pathogenic-Th17 mechanisms.
  • The epigenetic regulator CHD1 partners with STAT3 to co-bind the RORγt locus, locking in the pro-inflammatory state.
  • IL-23R signaling alters GR phosphorylation, blocking its nuclear entry and relieving GR suppression of inflammatory genes (companion to the homeostatic-Th17 paper above).

mRNA vaccines engage unconventional pathways in CD8⁺ T cell priming

Jo, Li, Thakur, … Murphy et al. · Nature, 15 Apr 2026

Key findings

  • mRNA-LNP vaccines do not require cDC1 cells or the WDFY4-dependent cross-presentation pathway for CD8⁺ priming — cDC1 and cDC2 cells act redundantly.
  • CD8⁺ T cells primed by either subset differ phenotypically but both drive anti-tumor responses and memory.
  • "Cross-dressing" — cDCs acquiring peptide–MHC-I from non-hematopoietic cells (type-I-IFN-dependent) — contributes substantially, possibly explaining responses to non-encoded antigens.

Guidance on the clinical management of HIV-1 persistent low-level viraemia on antiretroviral treatment: a scoping review and international Delphi consensus

Clemente, Stam, Elvstam, … Wensing et al. · The Lancet HIV, 11 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • Scoping review + modified Delphi consensus on managing persistent HIV-1 low-level viraemia (LLV), most often 50–1000 copies/mL.
  • Evidence for risk at 50–200 copies/mL is inconsistent, whereas 200–1000 copies/mL carries stronger risk of failure/resistance.
  • Proposes a viral-load-stratified framework: confirm, assess adherence/interactions, resistance testing (especially 200–1000), and individualized regimen optimization.

Systematic discovery of pro- and anti-HIV host factors in primary human CD4⁺ T cells

Rathore, Dugan, Thornton, … Marson et al. · Cell, 20 Apr 2026

Key findings

  • Orthogonal genome-wide CRISPR activation and knockout screens in primary CD4⁺ T cells map the functional HIV host-factor landscape.
  • CRISPRa uncovered potent antivirals PI16, PPID, SHISA3 and ITM2A; PI16 blocks entry, while PPID (Cyp40) binds capsid and reduces nuclear import.
  • Structural modeling and evolutionary analysis pinpoint the domains and residues required for PPID-mediated restriction.

5′ leader defects drive persistent HIV-1 viremia on long-term ART

Box, Camilo-Contreras, Dragoni, … Simonetti et al. · Nature Communications, 2026 (17:4725)

Key findings

  • In >50 participants, nonsuppressible plasma viremia on ART is driven by highly clonal HIV-1 RNA carrying 5′-leader defects clustered near the major splice donor.
  • The new CLAWS digital-PCR assay distinguishes intact vs defective 5′L RNA and recapitulates sequencing-based estimates.
  • Defective genomes emerge early after ART initiation and dominate over long-term therapy — making CLAWS a practical clinical/cure monitoring tool.

Leukemic stem cell subtypes determine venetoclax resistance and therapeutic vulnerabilities in AML

Waclawiczek, Leppä, Renders, … Trumpp et al. · Cell Stem Cell, 7 May 2026

Key findings

  • LSC profiling from >150 AML patients defines four subtypes mirroring hematopoietic lineage stages, with a BCL-2 vs MCL-1/BCL-xL "MAC-score."
  • Venetoclax resistance usually arises via plasticity to a MEP-LSC state that switches survival to BCL-xL; rare MoDe-LSCs in LAMP5⁺ AML also resist.
  • Subtyping improves risk stratification and points to subtype-specific therapy — BCL-xL inhibitors for MEP-LSCs, MEK1/2 inhibition for MoDe-LSCs.

A ketogenesis-ferroptosis axis maintains leukemic stem cell survival and leukemia progression

Han, Wang, Ma, … Jiang et al. · Cell Stem Cell, 11 May 2026

Key findings

  • AML leukemic stem cells run autonomous ketogenesis (fatty-acid oxidation + high HMGCS2) to produce β-hydroxybutyrate (BHB).
  • Deleting Hmgcs2 collapses BHB, disrupts LSC function and impairs leukemia progression while largely sparing normal hematopoiesis.
  • BHB suppresses ferroptosis by limiting pro-ferroptotic phospholipid remodeling via epigenetic FADS2 regulation — a targetable metabolic vulnerability.

Immunoediting restricts clonal neoantigens in primary, treatment-naive human tumors

Borden, Castellano, Kantzos, … Hastings et al. · Immunity, 11 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • In treatment-naive cutaneous SCC, high-immune-infiltrate tumors from immunocompetent patients carry lower overall and clonal mutational burdens (even after adjusting for purity and growth rate).
  • Predicted neoantigen–MHC-I binding affinity falls as variant allele frequency rises — restriction of MHC-binding neoantigens.
  • Immunogenic neoantigens are depleted in clonal vs subclonal cells: direct evidence of immunoediting in primary human cancer.

Dynamic transitioning between MAPK-driven and WNT-driven cell states drives intestinal cancer and shapes therapy response

Moore, Biehs, Kljavin, … de Sauvage et al. · Nature Genetics, 11 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • In an allele-specific RAS mouse model, KRAS drives a regenerative state but antagonizes Lgr5⁺ stem cells and cannot initiate intestinal tumors alone.
  • Initiation needs WNT activation (a stem-like state); growth uses a MAPK-driven transit-amplifying-like state.
  • This plasticity shapes drug response — explaining weaker KRAS-G12C-inhibitor effect in CRC vs NSCLC and arguing for dual targeting.

Treatment-related adverse events of CD3-based T cell-engaging bispecific antibodies in patients with cancer: a meta-analysis of clinical trials

Zhu, Liu, Rosen, Liu & Zhu · The Lancet Oncology, 12 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • Meta-analysis of 104 trials (10,353 patients) of CD3-based T-cell-engaging bispecific antibodies.
  • All-grade AEs were near-universal (~97–98%); grade ≥3 reached 70.3% in hematologic vs 45.3% in solid tumors; CRS was the most common all-grade event (43–46%).
  • Most frequent grade ≥3 events: neutropenia (heme) and raised γ-GT (solid); treatment-related death ~1.0%, mostly infections/organ failure.

Tumor cells metabolically resist immune-checkpoint therapy by macrophage efferocytosis-mediated fatty acid recycling

Liang, Long, Xiong, … Cheng et al. · Cancer Cell, 8 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • In checkpoint-blockade-resistant hepatocellular carcinoma, tumor cells exploit macrophage efferocytosis to counteract immunotherapy.
  • TREM2⁺ lipid-associated macrophages recycle fatty acids to tumor cells via efferocytosis-derived extracellular vesicles, driving H3K36-acetylation-linked MYC and TGF-β.
  • Myeloid Trem2 deletion or anti-TREM2 antibody abolishes the fuel supply and re-sensitizes tumors to ICB — a pan-cancer vulnerability.

Lin, Chen, Li, … Qu et al. · Nature Neuroscience, 16 Apr 2026

Triana-Martinez, Pierantoni, Graca, … Bulavin et al. · Immunity, 26 Mar 2026

Key findings

  • p16ʰⁱᵍʰ immune cells are critical mediators of disease tolerance — limiting tissue damage without changing pathogen burden.

NF-κB-activated fibroblasts orchestrate inflammaging and emergence of pro-inflammatory granzyme K⁺ T cells

Allen, Ringler, Woo, … Peng et al. · Immunity, 27 Mar 2026

Key findings

  • Age-dependent NF-κB activation in tissue fibroblasts remodels immune architecture, driving exhausted GZMK⁺CD8⁺ T cells seen in aging, autoimmunity and cancer.
  • A fibroblast–macrophage–T-cell circuit forms tertiary lymphoid structures in the lung.
  • It raises acute-lung-injury susceptibility (mimicking elderly pneumonia); depleting GZMK⁺ T cells alleviates it — a structural basis for inflammaging.

Neuroimmunology 1 paper

Single-cell proteome atlas of aging mouse microglia reveals subpopulation-specific phagoproteome

Zhang, Liu, Chen, … Li et al. · Neuron, 11 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • Label-free single-cell proteomics (Orbitrap Astral) profiled 3,085 microglia from young/middle-aged/aged mouse hippocampus and PFC (~1,153 proteins/cell) — one of the largest such datasets to date.
  • Higher expression completeness than transcriptomics with only moderate cross-modality correlation; reveals spatiotemporal proteomic heterogeneity during aging.
  • Defines a microglial "phagoproteome" with state-specific phagocytic preferences — a resource for studying microglial state transitions.

Innovations in Bioinformatics 1 paper

XomicsToModel: omics data integration and generation of thermodynamically consistent metabolic models

Preciat, Wegrzyn, Luo, Thiele, Hankemeier & Fleming · Nature Protocols, 1 Dec 2025

Key findings

  • A semi-automated pipeline that fuses bibliomic, transcriptomic, proteomic and metabolomic data with a generic genome-scale reconstruction to extract context-specific models.
  • Unlike prior extraction algorithms, it guarantees the output is stoichiometrically, thermodynamically and flux consistent.
  • Runs in MATLAB with basic constraint-based-modeling skills; applications span systems biology, drug development and personalized medicine.
