Basic Immunology 3 papers

Organ injury in systemic autoimmunity is mediated by stem-like CD8 T cells arising from tissue-draining lymph nodes

Al Souz, Wei, Cui, … Arazi, Ho, Craft et al. · Immunity, 20 Apr 2026

Key findings

  • TCF-1⁺ stem-like CD8 T cells in renal-draining lymph nodes undergo TCR-dependent, antigen-driven expansion in lupus-prone mice.
  • They differentiate into cytotoxic kidney-infiltrating cells that cause tissue injury, contingent on CD4 help plus IL-21 and IL-15 signaling.
  • Differentiation features persistent AP-1 activity and retained cytotoxicity despite rising immune-checkpoint expression.
  • A parallel CD8 differentiation program in human lupus-nephritis kidneys indicates shared pathogenesis with chronic infection and cancer.

Single-cell RNA sequencing of terminal ileal biopsies identifies signatures of Crohn's disease pathogenesis

Krzak, Alegbe, Taylor, … McIntyre, Raine, Anderson et al. · Nature Genetics, 15 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • IBDverse profiles >1.1 million cells from terminal ileal biopsies of 111 Crohn's patients and 232 controls, with discovery and replication cohorts.
  • Epithelium shows interferon-driven MHC class I upregulation that persists in progenitor cells even after macroscopic inflammation resolves.
  • ITGA4⁺ macrophages emerge as key inflammatory drivers, enriched for JAK-STAT signaling and IL-6, IL-12 and IL-23 expression.
  • Heritability analysis links inflammatory monocytes and macrophages to Crohn's susceptibility, implicating resident and recruited immune cells.

Interleukin-10 Autoantibodies and HLA-DRB1*01:03 in Inflammatory Bowel Disease

Gharahdaghi, Yeh, Ceron-Gutierrez, … Hambleton, Doffinger, Uhlig et al. · N Engl J Med, 11 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • Neutralizing IL-10 autoantibodies were detected in 173 of 4909 IBD patients (3.5%) and in none of 1006 controls.
  • High serum anti-IL-10 activity reduced detectable IL-10 and produced an exaggerated proinflammatory cytokine response (IL-23, IL-1β, TNF, IL-6).
  • Seropositivity was strongly associated with HLA-DRB1*01:03 (odds ratios ~25–50 across cohorts), the top genetic risk allele for ulcerative colitis.
  • The autoantibodies phenocopy monogenic IL-10 signaling defects, defining an autoimmune mechanism within a subgroup of IBD.

Cancer 3 papers

Neutrophil regulation of immunotherapy for cancer is controlled by type II interferon

Pei, Pan, Liang, … Ravetch, Soehnlein, Karlsson et al. · Immunity, 15 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • In neutropenic mice, the dominant neutrophil function was to block responses to T-cell– or myeloid-targeted immunotherapy.
  • Neutrophils upregulate PD-L1 on treatment; this depended on IFN-γ produced by cytotoxic lymphocytes and was cell-intrinsic (cd274/Ifngr1).
  • Without IFN-γ–driven suppression, neutrophils change phenotype to support rather than block immunotherapy.
  • Type II interferon (IFN-γ) is the switch determining whether neutrophils help or hinder cancer immunotherapy — and PD-L1 marks the suppressive pool.

H3K27me3 spreading organizes canonical PRC1 chromatin architecture to regulate developmental programs

Krug, Hu, Chen, … Majewski, Jabado, Lu et al. · Nature Genetics, 18 May 2026

Key findings

  • Polycomb-anchored chromatin looping is controlled by how far H3K27me3 spreads, which sets target-gene silencing and cell-fate choice.
  • Using glioma H3K27M mutations to restrict spreading, confinement concentrates canonical PRC1 and intensifies 3D interactions resembling pluripotency.
  • Conversely, spreading H3K27me3 dilutes local cPRC1 and weakens Polycomb loop contacts.
  • Disrupting cPRC1 binding or aggregation relaxes repression and drives differentiation and tumor regression of H3K27M-mutant glioma.

Inflammatory cytokines induce new cancer dependencies

Cheruiyot, Kim, Dubrot, … Bardeesy, Yates, Manguso et al. · Nature Genetics, 9 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • Genome-scale CRISPR loss-of-function screens across 8 cancer models under IFN-γ, IFN-β or TNF mapped inflammation-induced genetic vulnerabilities.
  • GPI transamidase complex subunits and the lipid phosphatase FITM2 emerged as interferon-specific cancer dependencies.
  • FITM2 loss predisposed cells to IFN-γ–driven ER and oxidative stress, culminating in paraptosis-like cell death.
  • Tumor-specific deletion of GPI transamidase subunits or FITM2 markedly enhanced response to immune checkpoint blockade in vivo.

Aging 1 paper

Plasma proteomic signatures of cellular aging predict human disease

Ding, Bot, Chen, … Isakova, Schott, Wyss-Coray et al. · Nature Medicine, 15 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • From 7,000+ plasma proteins in 60,542 individuals, machine-learning models estimate the biological age of 40+ cell types across many lineages.
  • 20–25% of people showed accelerated aging in a single cell type; cellular aging signatures predicted incident disease and mortality over 15 years.
  • APOE4 carriers had older astrocytes but younger macrophages; extreme astrocyte aging tripled Alzheimer's risk in APOE4 homozygotes.
  • Aged skeletal myocytes raised ALS risk 12.7-fold and aged respiratory epithelium added 58% lung-cancer risk in smokers; a polycellular score stratified mortality.

Neonatal / Pediatric Immunology 1 paper

The gut microbiome in preterm infants: development, dysbiosis, and disease implications

Tao, Wang & Yuan · Clinical Microbiology Reviews, 15 Jun 2026 · review

Key findings

  • Preterm gut assembly is shaped by immaturity, clinical interventions and the NICU, yielding low-diversity dysbiosis with Enterobacteriaceae expansion.
  • This dysbiosis drives necrotizing enterocolitis via TLR4 signaling, bile-acid dysmetabolism and immune dysregulation, with systemic effects through gut–brain, –lung and –eye axes.
  • Interventions span human-milk feeding and antibiotic stewardship to probiotics, postbiotics, phage therapy and fecal microbiota transplantation.
  • Multi-omics, machine learning and digital-twin modeling point toward personalized microbiome-mediated care, while antimicrobial resistance remains a hidden burden.

Resolving sensitivity, specificity and signal contamination in Xenium spatial transcriptomics

Bilous, Buszta, Bac, … Peters, Homicsko, Gottardo et al. · Nature Methods, 30 Apr 2026

Key findings

  • A 40+ breast and lung tumor-section Xenium resource dissects technical noise, transcript spillover, assay specificity, panel performance and segmentation.
  • Single-nucleus RNA-seq enables precise quantification of transcript contamination, providing a benchmark for Xenium performance.
  • SPLIT (Spatial Purification of Layered Intracellular Transcripts) resolves mixed transcriptomic signals to improve background correction and cell-type resolution.
  • Purified signal reveals T-cell exhaustion signatures linked to malignant-cell colocalization that would otherwise remain obscured.

A human induced pluripotent stem cell model for the holistic study of epithelial-to-mesenchymal transitions

Hookway, Borensztejn, Harris, … Theriot, Gunawardane, Rafelski et al. · Nature Methods, 15 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • hiPS cells in defined geometries — 2D colonies and 3D lumenoids — enable multimodal measurement of EMT dynamics on a single platform.
  • Fixed- and live-cell imaging quantify migration, EMT markers, cell–cell junctions and basement-membrane interactions during induced EMT.
  • Cell-culture geometry shapes the timing of migration onset, which is quantitatively linked to basement-membrane integrity.
  • The work establishes a standardized imaging framework, with accessible datasets and tools, for analyzing cell-state transitions.

Purine bias in bacterial genes is driven by runaway transcription

Dierksheide, Taggart, Johnson & Li · Nature Microbiology, 15 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • A large-scale reporter assay in Bacillus subtilis shows purine-rich (A/G) gene content is critical for gene expression.
  • In species with "runaway transcription," RNA polymerases outpace ribosomes, leaving nascent mRNA exposed to the termination factor Rho.
  • Purine bias diverges from Rho's target specificity, preventing premature transcription termination; lineages that lost Rho lack the bias.
  • This constraint underlies codon-usage biases, suppresses pyrimidine-rich antisense transcripts, and shapes adaptation of foreign genes.
