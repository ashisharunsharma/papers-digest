Basic Immunology 3 papers

The gut microbiota directs vitamin A flux to regulate intestinal T cell development

Srinivasan, Dende, Ruhn, … McDonald, Koh, Hooper et al. · Cell Host & Microbe, 17 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • The microbiota moves retinoids (retinol, retinoic acid) along a sequential epithelium → myeloid → T-cell relay reaching mesenteric lymph nodes over ~3 days.
  • Microbe-associated molecular patterns induce epithelial serum amyloid A proteins, which are necessary and sufficient for epithelial-to-myeloid retinoid transfer and myeloid migration.
  • In the mLN, microbial antigen drives retinoid hand-off from myeloid cells to developing T cells, triggering RAR-dependent transcriptional programming.
  • The pathway is activated during postnatal development, the window when gut adaptive immunity is first established.

Common γ-chain cytokines induce an epigenomically plastic precursor-like KIT⁺ILC2 state linked to immune disease susceptibility

Olsthoorn, Onrust-Van Schoonhoven, de Bruijn, … Di Santo, Hendriks, Stadhouders et al. · J Allergy Clin Immunol, 17 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • KIT⁺ ILC2s maintain a hybrid identity, co-expressing and keeping open chromatin at both ILC-precursor and ILC2 gene programs.
  • Their epigenome is poised at ILC3 effector loci (IL17, IL23R) plus naïve-lymphocyte and tissue-homing genes, explaining their phenotypic plasticity.
  • Common γc cytokines IL-2/IL-7 induce and maintain the KIT⁺ state through STAT5 activation.
  • Genetic risk variants for asthma and autoimmunity are enriched in this poised epigenome, nominating STAT5 signaling for therapeutic targeting.

Translation-dependent degradation of cas12 mRNA triggered by an anti-CRISPR

Marino, Talaie, Gerovac, … Swaney, Vogel, Bondy-Denomy et al. · Nature, 29 Apr 2026

Key findings

  • The phage anti-CRISPR AcrVA2 inhibits Cas12a biogenesis rather than blocking DNA binding or cleavage.
  • AcrVA2 binds conserved residues near the Cas12a N-terminus and triggers selective degradation of cas12a mRNA during its translation.
  • Its C-terminal domain co-sediments with ribosomes and polysomes — a step required for targeted co-translational mRNA decay.
  • The C-domain is broadly conserved in homologs found in hosts lacking cas12a, suggesting a general mechanism for gene regulation and molecular conflict in bacteria.

A mechanosensitive lipolytic factor in the bone marrow promotes osteogenesis and lymphopoiesis

Peng, Hu, Xie, … Huang, Jiang, Luo et al. · Cell Metabolism, 18 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • Identifies a bone-marrow lipolytic factor whose secretion is mechanosensitive, responding to mechanical/skeletal load.
  • By driving lipolysis of marrow adipocytes, the factor mobilizes lipid-derived energy to support the niche.
  • Its activity promotes osteogenesis (bone formation) while simultaneously boosting marrow lymphopoiesis.
  • Establishes a mechano-metabolic axis coupling physical load and fat metabolism to skeletal and immune-cell renewal. (Record retrieved as a published correction to the original Cell Metabolism article.)

The role of short-chain fatty acids as key mediators of gut microbiota–host crosstalk in thyroid diseases

Shen, Zhang, Qi · Autoimmunity Reviews, 19 Jun 2026 (review)

Key findings

  • Thyroid-disease patients commonly show gut dysbiosis with fewer SCFA-producing bacteria and lower systemic SCFA levels.
  • SCFAs regulate immune and metabolic homeostasis via GPCR signaling, HDAC inhibition, mitochondrial metabolism, mTOR-S6K signaling, and barrier protection.
  • SCFA deficiency may break immune tolerance, promoting autoimmunity and tumor progression in the thyroid.
  • Targeting SCFA pathways with probiotics, prebiotics or microbiota transplantation is a promising but still largely correlative therapeutic avenue needing causal validation.

HIV 1 paper

Vaccination generates broadly cross-neutralizing antibodies to the HIV Env apex

Guenaga, Ádori, Bale, … Ozorowski, Karlsson Hedestam, Wyatt et al. · Nature, 29 Apr 2026

Key findings

  • Heterologous HIV Env trimers covalently coupled to liposomes for multivalent display were used as an immunogen in non-human primates.
  • All trimer-liposome-immunized macaques developed cross-neutralizing HIV serum antibody responses.
  • Monoclonal antibodies isolated from multiple macaques cross-neutralized divergent HIV clinical isolates.
  • Cryo-EM showed these mAbs target the Env trimer apex in a manner highly similar to the human-derived apex bNAb PG9 — a notable advance for HIV vaccine design.

Spatial distribution of the proteome in the human body and in cancers

Yue, Jiang, Li, … Ji, Li, Guo et al. · Nature, 17 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • DIA mass spectrometry profiled >13,000 proteins across 2,856 samples spanning 58 tissue types, 251 subtypes and 25 carcinomas.
  • The map traces spatially resolved proteome trajectories across fetal, tumour, adjacent non-tumour and healthy adult states.

  • The resource prioritizes therapeutic targets for common cancers, bridging developmental and oncogenic biology.

Expansion and CAR engineering of granulocyte-monocyte progenitors for cellular immunotherapy

Yue, Guo, Pan, … Majeti, Zhang, Ying et al. · Cell, 19 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • Defined culture conditions enable long-term expansion of mouse and human GMPs while preserving progenitor identity and myeloid potential; myeloperoxidase regulates their proliferation.
  • Transferred GMPs seed hematopoietic niches, generate donor-derived myelopoiesis, restore antibacterial defense in chronic granulomatous disease mice, and yield abundant tumor-infiltrating macrophages.
  • CAR-engineered GMPs suppress CD19⁺ leukemia and HER2⁺ solid tumors.
  • A CAR with an IgG-Fc domain recruits host Fc-receptor phagocytes, enables T-cell priming across MHC mismatch, and boosts efficacy in immunocompetent allogeneic models.

Liu, Noguera-Ortega, Dong, … Moon, Albelda, Huh et al. · Nature Biotechnology, 17 Oct 2025

Key findings

Aging 1 paper

Age and early life adversity shape heterogeneity of the epigenome across tissues in macaques

Sadoughi, Petersen, Patterson, … Platt, Snyder-Mackler, Lea et al. · Science, 18 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • DNA methylation profiled across 14 tissues in 237 semi-free-ranging rhesus macaques with naturally occurring early-life adversity (ELA).
  • Age-associated methylation was predominantly tissue-dependent, yet epigenetic aging was relatively consistent within each individual.
  • ELA effects were adversity-specific but exerted coordinated effects across tissues, often hitting the same loci as age — but in opposite directions.
  • Thus ELA does not uniformly increase epigenetic age; it leaves a distinct, coordinated cross-tissue signature intertwined with age-related change.

Neuroimmunology 3 papers

A fatty acid amide activates myeloid cells and improves neurovascular outcomes in retinal degeneration

Wei, Chatterjee, Yang, … Sailor, Boger, Friedlander et al. · Nature Neuroscience, 19 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • An unbiased metabolomics screen found erucamide, a 22:1 ω-9 monounsaturated fatty-acid amide, highly dysregulated during photoreceptor degeneration in mice.
  • Erucamide delivered by porous-silicon nanoparticles activated retinal myeloid cells, upregulating angiogenic and neurotrophic cytokines.
  • This limited both vascular and neuronal degeneration, protecting the neurovascular unit.
  • TMEM19 was identified as the erucamide binding protein required for myeloid activation and neuroprotection, defining a new fatty-acid-amide neuroimmune pathway and candidate therapeutics.

Tryptamine from wake-active monoaminergic neurons regulates sleep homeostasis

Cao, Wang, Zhao, … Hu, Yuan, Zhang et al. · Nature Neuroscience, 19 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • Cerebrospinal-fluid tryptamine (TrpA) tracks homeostatic sleep pressure in nocturnal mice and diurnal pigs, reflecting physical-activity history independent of the light-dark cycle.
  • TrpA is produced by wake-active monoaminergic nuclei in the diencephalon and brainstem and secreted in an activity-dependent manner.
  • Released TrpA binds GPR139 and enhances excitability of hypothalamic preoptic-area neurons to promote sleep.
  • TrpA–GPR139 signaling is necessary for homeostatic sleep rebound, and small-molecule GPR139 agonists increase sleep duration and quality — a druggable target.

Plasticity and language in the anaesthetized human hippocampus

Katlowitz, Cole, Mickiewicz, … Rungratsameetaweemana, Hayden, Sheth et al. · Nature, 6 May 2026

Key findings

  • High-density Neuropixels recordings show hippocampal neurons and local oscillations retain oddball-tone detection during anaesthesia-induced loss of consciousness.
  • The effect grew over ~10 minutes, demonstrating representational plasticity, reproduced by a biologically plausible recurrent neural network.
  • With language stimuli, single units and local field potentials carried semantic and grammatical information about natural speech.
  • Activity even predicted semantic information about upcoming words — complex sensory processing in the hippocampus persists in the unconscious state.

Neonatal / Pediatric Immunology 1 paper

Maternal trans-vaccenic acid shapes neonatal T cell development and early-life immune imprinting

Fan, Zheng, Oliphant, … Claud, He, Chen et al. · Science, 18 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • Maternal supplementation with trans-vaccenic acid (TVA), the predominant natural trans-fatty acid in breast milk, promoted neonatal T cell development in mice.
  • TVA-fed neonates showed an expanded naïve CD4 T cell pool and enhanced adaptive immunity against infection.
  • TVA reprogrammed neonatal naïve CD4 T cells via a GPCR–CTCF axis and drove Th1 skewing by cooperating with the transcription factor TBX21.
  • Early-life maternal TVA exposure via breastfeeding supported long-lasting antiviral immunity into adulthood.

Innovations in Bioinformatics 4 papers

Whole-organ spatial transcriptional analysis at cellular resolution using TRISCO

Li, Walton, Kreutzmann, … Hecksher-Sørensen, Kanatani, Uhlén et al. · Nature Protocols, 17 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • TRISCO (Tris-buffer-mediated retention of in-situ HCR signal in cleared organs) enables single-cell 3D RNA mapping across whole tissue volumes.
  • It delivers homogeneous, well-preserved labeling throughout intact organs — brain, lung, heart, kidney and spinal cord — in mouse, rat and guinea pig.
  • The protocol avoids complex instrumentation, specialized expertise and harsh chemical treatments, broadening accessibility.
  • The 10–15 day workflow has flexible stopping points and suits labs already equipped for light-sheet microscopy, advancing whole-organ 3D histology of RNA.

Atlas of human brain imaging-derived phenotypes and disease risk

Liu, Guo, Yan, … Deng, Wang, Yu et al. · Med, 17 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • A phenome-wide association study linked 505 brain imaging-derived phenotypes (IDPs) to 756 incident diseases across 15 organ systems in 64,836 UK Biobank participants.
  • Across ~380,000 tests, 1,500 significant IDP–disease pairs showed brain alterations relate to circulatory, digestive and metabolic disorders, not only neurological ones.
  • White-matter-related IDPs emerged as a central hub linking brain and multisystem health.
  • Mendelian randomization suggested potential causal roles of white-matter IDPs in cerebrovascular disorders; all results are open via brainphewas.com.

Expanding the human proteome with microproteins and peptideins

Deutsch, Kok, Mudge, … Moritz, Prensner, van Heesch et al. (TransCODE Consortium) · Nature, 6 May 2026

Key findings

  • About 25% of 7,264 non-canonical ORFs yielded detectable peptides across a meta-analysis of 95,520 proteomics experiments.
  • The work codifies ncORF-encoded microproteins as human proteins and introduces "peptideins" — microproteins of indeterminate functional potential.
  • A new evolutionary metric, ORF relative branch length (ORBL), shows evolutionary constraint is common and associates with peptide detection.
  • One peptidein from the OLMALINC lncRNA has a pan-essential cellular phenotype; tools are released via GENCODE and PeptideAtlas.

Plasma proteomic signature of the human menstrual cycle

Riishede, Rode, Lundegaard, … Pinborg, Stender, Ghouse et al. · Nature Medicine, 13 Apr 2026

Key findings

  • Profiling ~3,000 plasma proteins in 2,760 UK Biobank women identified 198 proteins that vary across the menstrual cycle.
  • These form distinct temporal patterns aligned with menstrual phases and include reproductive hormones, cytokines and growth factors enriched in endometrium.
  • Several proteins were linked to common reproductive disorders — endometriosis, leiomyoma and abnormal bleeding.
  • A 75-protein proteomic score accurately predicts cycle phase, providing a systems-level atlas for women's-health biomarker discovery.

AI Applications in Basic Science Research 1 paper

Cellular architecture and neighborhood-informed virtual spatial tumor profiling from histopathology

Li, Li, Quinton, … Neal, Diehn, Li et al. · Cell, 16 Jun 2026

Key findings

  • CANVAS is an AI platform that infers tumor ecological habitats directly from H&E histopathology.
  • Built on an atlas of >18 million cells profiled by 41-plex spatial proteomics across 457 NSCLC patients, it defines 10 reproducible cellular neighborhoods capturing conserved TME organization.
  • Via multimodal alignment and foundation-model morphological encoding, it predicts CN-anchored habitat structures from H&E and was evaluated in >5,000 patients across 9 cancer types.
  • CANVAS supports prognostic modeling, spatial ecotype stratification and immunotherapy outcome prediction — a clinically scalable spatial-profiling platform.
